PIAIC

PRESIDENTIAL INITIATIVE FOR ARTIFICAL INTELLIGENCE AND CLOUD COMPUTING

The Presidential Initiative for Artificial Intelligence and Computing (PIAIC) was launched by the President of Pakistan, Dr. Arif Alvi, to promote education, research and business opportunities in Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain, Internet of Things, and Cloud Native Computing. The initiative comes in a bid to enable Pakistan in making an imprint on the world's path towards the Fourth Industrial Revolution.It aims to transform the fields of :

  • Cloud Native Computing
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Block Chain
  • Internet of Things

Cloud Native Computing

The Cloud Native Computing program prepares the students to program in open source technologies like, Linux,Docker,Kubernates,Javascript,Node.js , React and react Native while introducing the students to development of Microservices and software development techniques on cloud platforms like AWS.

Artificial Intelligence

The Artificial Intelligence program of PIAIC takes the student through the foundation of the AI technology , via teaching programming in open source like Python, Numpy, Pandas,Tensorflow 2.0 and Flash and teaching deploying practical AI solutions and use cases ans ending with real-life example of modern day AI in practice.

Block Chain

The main emphasis of Blockchain program is to understand the blockchains like Ethereum and Hyperledger(fabric), and learn to program in languages such as Solidity,Javascript on frameworks like Node.js. The program also introduce them to development of Distributed Apps(DApps),frameworks like web3 and Truffle and an in-depth understanding of Blockchain-as-a-service(BaaS) and how to develop and offer an initial Coin Offering (ICO) of a cryto-token

Internet Of Things:

The Internet of Things program introduces the students toopen source development technologies like Rust, EMbedded Rust,WebAssembly,Hyper,Diesel,Google Assistant and Kubernates while making them efficient in developing Micro Services for IoT Edge,IoT Cloud, and voice computing for device control
